Bio

I am a PhD student from Mondragon University in Spain, currently working in the Industrial Cybersecurity Team at IKERLAN in The Basque Country, Spain.

Research Interests

My research focuses on system security, more precisely in security testing methodologies for embedded systems and metrics development. The research questions I aim to answer at the end of my thesis are:

  • Do the contrameasures deployed work properly?
  • Is their coverage enough to undermine the threats?
  • Are they implemented correctly?

The most interesting point in my research will be the development of security metrics. Available methodologies don't usually propose metrics to know the level of security reached with all the controls and countermeasures. And when metrics are proposed, they are qualitative or the way to obtain a final value is not clearly explained nor can be applied to embedded systems. You can download the last version of my thesis poster.

Because of my background, I usually work on my own projects about electronics. I am passionate about designing and building circuits, PCBs, vacuum tubes and solid state amplifiers and anything of this kind.
